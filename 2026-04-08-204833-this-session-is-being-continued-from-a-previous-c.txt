  This is a proper knowledge graph — concepts as nodes, resources as nodes,
  weighted edges between them. Most competitors have flat tags at best.

  What's Missing to Unlock the Next Level

  1. Temporal signals — You don't track when or how a user interacts with
  resources after saving. Adding a resourceView table (resourceId, userId,
  viewedAt, durationMs) would enable:
  - "Pick up where you left off"
  - Trending/fading topics in your library
  - "You haven't revisited this in 3 months but saved 5 related items since"

  2. User-level embeddings / interest profile — A rolling embedding of what the
  user saves over time. This enables proactive recommendations ("based on your
  recent saves, you might want to explore X"). Could be computed as a weighted
  average of recent resource embeddings.

  3. Cross-resource content chunks — Right now embeddings are per-resource. For
  AI Q&A over your library, you need chunked content with per-chunk embeddings
  so you can retrieve specific passages, not just whole documents. A
  resourceChunk table (resourceId, content, embedding, chunkIndex) would enable
  RAG.

  4. Conversation/thread history — If you add AI Q&A ("ask your library"),
  you'll want to store conversation threads so users can refine questions and
  the AI has context.

  5. Highlights/annotations — User-created highlights on resources (text ranges,
   notes). These are high-signal for what matters to the user and could feed
  into the interest profile.

  6. Action items / tasks extraction — The enricher could extract TODOs and
  action items from content. A lightweight task layer linked to source
  resources.

  Verdict

  For semantic search, related resources, connection discovery, smart 
  collections, and auto-tagging — you're already set. The infrastructure is
  there, it just needs frontend surfaces.

  For AI Q&A / RAG — you need content chunking. That's the biggest gap.

  For personalization / proactive features — you need interaction tracking.
  Simple to add, high leverage.

  Want me to start with any of these additions, or focus on surfacing the AI
  data you already have (semantic search UI, graph view, etc)?
